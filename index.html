Instructions

Welcome to the Buy & Hold Portfolio Calculator! This tool helps you calculate how much you should contribute or withdraw to each of the assets in your portfolio during a contribution, withdrawal, or rebalance of your portfolio. The following are some tips on using this tool.

Basic usage:

  1. To perform a rebalance of your portfolio, go to the "Rebalance?" option and toggle the switch to the right (). If you instead would like to calculate a contribution or withdrawal without rebalancing your portfolio, toggle the switch to the left ().
  2. Choose whether you would like to contribute or withdraw money into/from your portfolio by selecting either the "CONTRIBUTE" or "WITHDRAW" button. The selection will be red. Note that you are also allowed to make a contribution or withdrawal during a portfolio rebalance. If you do not plan to make a contribution/withdrawal, you may use either option and set the "amount" to zero (you may do this when rebalancing).
  3. In the "amount" text box, set the amount you would like to contribute or withdraw from your portfolio (contribution/withdrawal determined by the button selected above). You may set this value to zero if you do not plan to contribute or withdraw any money.
  4. If you selected "CONTRIBUTE", you will also be given the option to choose between the "TARGETED" and "TRADITIONAL" strategy (red is the selected option).
    • A "targeted" strategy tries to minimize the amount of capital gains tax if/when you next rebalance your portfolio. It does this by contributing to assets that are below your desired asset mix and possibly selling tax-exempt assets.
    • The "traditional" contribution strategy will contribute amounts to each of your assets that is proportional to their asset mix. It also bases this decision on your contribution history for a given desired asset mix.
    Next to these strategy buttons is an info button that when clicked gives more detailed information about these strategies. It is up to you to decide which of these strategies and results best suit you, if any.
  5. Below the "amount" field if you selected "WITHDRAW" or the strategy buttons if you selected "CONTRIBUTE", there is a list of your assets. If this is the first time, there will be only one unnamed asset in the list. You can edit this asset by clicking the or button. Note that the indicates that the asset is incomplete or has errors and must be edited before performing any calculations. After you click on the button, a dialog window will pop up titled "Edit Asset". All fields must be filled out:
    • "Ticker symbol" is the ticker symbol of the asset. Note that if you include cash in your portfolio, you must use the special "$cash" ticker symbol (which is suggested when you click on the "Ticker symbol" field). You may put any name you like in here but duplicates are not allowed.
    • "Taxable?" when activated (switch to the right) indicates that the asset is taxable. When deactivated (switched to the left) the asset is tax-exempt.
    • "Mutual fund?" when activated (switch to the right) indicates that the asset is a mutual fund or similar asset where you do not need to buy full shares. When deactivated (switch to the left) the asset must be bought in whole shares like stocks or exchange traded funds.
      • If you activated the "Mutual fund?" switch, there will be a "Balance" field where you should enter your present balance for that asset.
      • If you deactivated the "Mutual fund?" switch, there will be a "Shares" field and a "Order price/share" field. The "Shares" field is the number of (possibly fractional) shares you own of the asset. The "Order price/share" is the price you would like to pay per share for new shares of this asset.
    • "Desired asset mix" is the percentage of your portfolio that you would like allocated to this asset. Across all assets, this percentage must add up to 100%. To help with this, a panel will drop down telling your the remaining percentage you need to allocate.
    • When finished editing the asset, click the "CLOSE" button at the bottom right of the box.
  6. An asset may be deleted from the list by clicking on the button which will dropdown two buttons: a "Delete" button that will confirm deletion of the asset and a "Cancel" button that cancel deletion of the asset.
  7. Below the asset list, you will see a "red" button called "ADD ASSET". Clicking on this will add another asset to the above list and automatically open an editing window.
  8. To compute a contribution, withdrawal, or a rebalance, you finally click on the "CALCULATE" button below which will display the following results:
    • For contributions/withdrawals, this will compute your contribution or withdrawal (depending on whether "CONTRIBUTE" or "WITHDRAW" is selected above). The suggested amount to buy or sell from each asset is displayed on the right side of each asset in the above asset list. An overall status of the calculation is displayed to the right above the asset list.
    • For a rebalance, this button will perform a rebalance and will report the suggested actions for each asset in the same way as detailed above in the above step.
  9. After clicking the "CALCULATE" button and if you had selected the "TRADITIONAL" contribution strategy, a panel will appear from the bottom of the page with a "Last cycled" field and a "CYCLE" button. "Cycling" is needed for the traditional contribution strategy because calculations depend on your contribution history for a given desired asset mix. For more info on the justification for this button, click the info button next to the CYCLE button. Here is the usage of the CYCLE button:
    • Do not click the CYCLE button until you have finished making your contribution. That is, if you have not actually made the recommended contribution to your real portfolio, you probably should not click the CYCLE button.
    • If you have actually made the recommeded contribution to your real portfolio, you may then click the CYCLE button. You do not need to click it immediately and can defer until your next visit. Just make sure you click the CYCLE button before you calculate your next contribution.
    • If you forget whether you cycled or not, the "Last cycled" field tells you the date and time of the last time you clicked the CYCLE button for this desired asset mix. If you have never cycled for this desired asset mix before, it will say "Never".
    • Note that if you modify your desired asset mix, the modified desired asset mix has its own history. Your browser storage will still keep the history of your previous desired asset mix.
    • If you accidentally click the CYCLE button before taking the last recommendation, you can just accept the new recommendation. In the long run, it does not make a major difference since the recommendations are cyclic. It is best to avoid forgetting too often however since it will bias your contributions.
    • The data stored by the CYCLE button has no impact on any other calculation besides traditional contributions.

Other activities

  • For convenience, this tool remembers your assets and some other information that you entered in the page when you return to the page. If you would like to delete this information, you can select "Clear data".
  • Since your data is persisted in your browser and is not stored on our server, you can export your data to transfer it to a differet browser/computer using "Export data". This will save a file "portfolio-data.json" to your downloads folder. You may also want to do this if you want to keep your data before clearing your data from the browser as above.
  • You can import data that you previously exported by "Import data".

Tips

  • Any numerical field may be treated as a calculator. You may compose mathematical expressions using (, ), +, -, *, /, along with numbers that will be evaluated once you leave the text field.
